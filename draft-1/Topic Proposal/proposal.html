FitHea: A Unified Fitness and Diet Tracking System

Problem/Data Domain

In recent years, maintaining a healthy lifestyle through fitness and diet has become an increasingly important goal for many people. However, these two facets of the health domain are often viewed as independent realms rather than as interconnected components within a unified system. Consequently, users frequently struggle to determine whether their dietary choices effectively support their three primary fitness goals: fat loss, muscle gain, or maintaining overall health.

The core information problem lies in the fragmentation of health-related data and the lack of integration between different types of user-generated information. Existing fitness and diet applications, such as MyFitnessPal or Fitbod, often focus on a specific aspect of health management, such as calorie tracking or exercise planning, but rarely offer an integrated system that seamlessly connects the two. This fragmentation makes it difficult for users to gain a holistic understanding of their data, leading to confusion, inconsistent tracking habits, and ultimately undermining the long-term engagement of users motivated to improve their fitness.

Therefore, we believe there is an urgent need for an interactive system capable of integrating fitness and dietary data, in order to help users better understand the correlation between their behaviors and health outcomes. A more integrated and user-centric solution would help enhance the system's usability and improve the effectiveness of long-term health management.

App Description

For many people, eating healthy and exercising regularly are closely related goals, but they are often managed separately in practice. Some users track workouts, while others focus on calorie intake, yet it can still be difficult to tell whether these choices are actually supporting the same outcome. FitHea is designed to address this gap by bringing fitness and diet into one place, so users can better understand how their daily habits connect to their overall health goals.

The application is intended for users with different goals, including fat loss, muscle gain, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Rather than offering a one-size-fits-all experience, FitHea begins by asking users to select one primary goal. This choice shapes how information is organized and what kinds of guidance are emphasized throughout the app. For example, a user focused on fat loss may see more attention given to calorie balance, while a user aiming for muscle gain may be guided toward foods with higher protein content. In this way, the app keeps the experience simple while still making it feel more relevant and personalized.

To support users in making everyday decisions, FitHea provides guidance through a small curated set of common foods, each with basic nutritional information such as calories and protein. Instead of relying on a large database or overly complex calculations, the app is designed to offer clear and manageable guidance that fits within ordinary routines. Users can browse recommended foods, compare options, and generate a simple summary of their selections. This summary can also be shared with friends, adding a small social element to the experience. By combining goal selection, food guidance, and lightweight feedback, FitHea offers a more practical and connected way for users to manage their health habits over time.

Features

1. Customize Goal Setter

When users first access the app, they choose one of three main goals, weight loss, muscle gain and stay healthy.

  • Interaction: When the user selects a goal and presses the 'Save' button, the layout of the dashboard and the data evaluation criteria change immediately.
  • Data Utilization: The user's selection values are stored in the database and are maintained on the next connection.

2. Integrated Nutrition and Activity Log

Manage food intake and exercise records in one interface.

  • Interaction: When you enter the name and quantity of food you eat, the system calculates and adds calories and nutrients to the list. Also, when you enter the exercise you performed, the calories burned will be automatically added up.
  • Data Utilization: Full summary data is updated in real time when users delete or modify items from the list.

2.5. Social Sharing and Community Support

Share your achievement rate with friends.

  • Interaction: Press the share button to share the current summary data to other Social media platforms.
